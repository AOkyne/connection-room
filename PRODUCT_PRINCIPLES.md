# Product Principles

Short, plain-language guidance for anyone working on The Connection Room —
designers, developers, copywriters, and Trevor himself. When a feature
decision is unclear, these principles should settle it before a technical
constraint does.

## People before features

Every feature should deepen connection rather than increase engagement for
engagement's sake. If a feature only exists to bring people back more
often, that's not a good enough reason on its own.

## Depth over volume

Meaningful conversations matter more than activity counts. We are not
optimizing for post counts, streaks, or time-in-app.

## Curiosity over performance

Members should feel invited to explore, not pressured to impress. Nothing
here should feel like an audition.

## Privacy creates choice, not emptiness

Members should be able to show enough of themselves to be known, while
keeping full control over what feels vulnerable. A profile that hides
everything by default isn't safer — it's just empty.

## Identity can be visible

Age, orientation, relationship status, location, and reasons for joining
are not automatically secrets. Members decide what they share; the default
should lean toward a real, human profile.

## Vulnerability should be chosen

Quiz results, reflections, growth edges, and other intimate personal
information should always be optional to share, never required or
default-shown.

## Safety information stays private

Boundaries, moderation reports, blocks, and account details should never
become profile content, no matter how it might otherwise be framed as
"interesting" or "helpful to share."

## The dashboard is a companion, not a control panel

The home experience should feel calm, guided, and human — one thing to sit
with today, not a dashboard of metrics and unread badges competing for
attention.

## Technology should disappear

The interface should support connection without calling attention to
itself. If a member notices the UI more than the person they're talking to,
something's off.

## Invitation over pressure

Avoid manipulative engagement tactics: aggressive streaks, shame-based
reminders, fake urgency, or guilt-driven nudges. Invite people back; don't
guilt them back.

## Community over dating-app dynamics

The product should support affinity, discovery, and connection without
reducing members to swipeable profiles. This is not a dating platform, and
it should never start to feel like one by accident.

## The paint is still wet

Founding members help shape the culture, language, and future direction of
the community. Treat current features and copy as a strong first draft, not
a finished product — expect this document itself to keep evolving with it.
